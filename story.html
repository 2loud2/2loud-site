2Loud

I have commotionitus.

It's not a real condition. But the problem is.

The made-up condition

I tell friends I have "commotionitus." It's my word for hating loud, chaotic environments. Restaurants where you can't hear the person across from you. Action movies that feel like they're attacking you. Sirens, alarms, sudden anything.

The condition isn't real. But the feeling is. And I'm not the only one who has it — I'm just the one who gave it a name.

Then there's Gina.

Gina is my dog. She's reactive. Every doorbell, gunshot, or siren that comes through the TV triggers barking, stress, and a ruined evening. Movie night becomes a game of hovering over the remote, trying to mute before the next loud moment.

And it's not just us. Parents spend 40 minutes getting a baby down, only to have a TV gunshot wake them up. People with PTSD avoid entire genres of film. People with misophonia can't sit through a meal scene. The problem is everywhere — it just doesn't have a product yet.

The question that started it

One night I was watching something and a sudden scream came through the speakers. Gina lost it. I fumbled for the remote. And I thought: why isn't there something that just... catches these sounds before they get to me?

I looked. There wasn't. There are apps that warn you about content. There are websites that list triggers by timestamp. But nothing that sits between you and the sound in real time and just quietly turns it down.

So I started building it.

I'm not a programmer.

I should be honest about that. I don't have a CS degree. I'm not an engineer. I have a full-time job that has nothing to do with this. I'm building 2Loud with AI-assisted tools, learning as I go, and figuring it out in public.

I think that's actually the point. The technology to do this has existed for years. Nobody built it — not because it's hard, but because the people who could build it didn't need it. I need it. My dog needs it. A lot of people need it.

So here I am, building the thing I went looking for and couldn't find.

Me and Gina.

That's the story so far.
